Favorite Food

Burritos are my favorite food as they're an entire meal served in a neat wrap which you can eat for any meal. My favorite breakfast burritos are simple with egg and cheese with a bit of hot sauce. For other times, my preferred Chipotle order is a vegetarian fajita burrito with black beans, mild salsa, red sauce, cheese and lettuce.

My Favorite Books

Book Name Cover Author Summary
Catcher in the Rye JD Salinger Catcher in the Rye is a classic coming-of-age story about the protagonist, Holden Caulfield, who spends an eventful weekend in New York City away from his preporatory school. When I first read it in high school at a school similar to Holden's, I thought he was cool and the book, in part, inspired me to move to New York City. Reading it as an adult, however, offers a totally different experience in which Holden is more naive boy than adventurer.
Money Changes Everything William Goetzmann As much as a historic accounting of currency throughout human history can be, Money Changes Everything is a page-turner that chronicles a fascinating account of how humans began to use different forms of currency (shells, precious metals, paper fiat, crypto, etc.) to denote value. Money Changes Everything starts with the early days of cavepeople and fast forwards to modern times highlighting the necessity of money and how it has helped create civilized society.
1984 George Orwell Another classic, 1984, provides a prescient view of near-future dystopian society in which citizens' activities are closely monitored my a centralized authority, language and history changed to engender thinking in support of the government and thoughts can be illegal. It remains incredibly relevant for modern day, especially in a formal professional role of working in data analytics for a public relations agency.
Thinking, Fast and Slow Daniel Kahneman Kahneman, a celebrated psychologist for his work in behavioral economics, introduces a number of concepts that help explain how human beings make decisions, including System 1 vs System 2 thinking, framing and loss aversion, among others. As someone who works in data for marketing use cases, it's a very relevant read into why people buy the things they do.
